Part One: Device Concept

The device is a smart electronic unit that can be attached to a marine animal, such as a shark, by securing it to the dorsal fin using a rust-resistant metal clip or a plastic screw. It can also be mounted on a small boat. Its purpose is to collect key environmental data (temperature, depth, movement, and GPS location), which can then be transmitted to researchers or stored locally. This innovation is designed to help scientists track marine life movements and better understand their natural habitats.

Part Two: Main Functions of the Device

  • Measuring temperature.
  • Measuring depth (through pressure).
  • Recording movement and directions.
  • Determining GPS location.
  • Transmitting data (via GSM or LoRa).
  • Storing data (SD Card).
  • Power management using a battery and voltage regulator.

Part Three: Overall Working Principle

The device uses an Arduino UNO as its central controller, which gathers signals from the sensors (LM35 and BMP280 for temperature and pressure, ADXL345 for movement, and GPS Neo-6M for location). The Arduino processes this data and converts it into digital values. The information is then either transmitted through the SIM800L module over mobile networks or via LoRa SX1278 in remote areas without coverage. At the same time, a backup copy of the data is stored on an SD Card.

The system is powered by a Li-ion battery with an AMS1117-3.3 voltage regulator to ensure stable power supply. With this setup, the device forms an integrated system that collects, processes, and transmits data in real time, supporting marine research and ecological studies.
